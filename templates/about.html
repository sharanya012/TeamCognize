Comprehensive Documentation of Insurance Policy Categories, Terms, and Costs

How Car Insurance Works:

Car insurance requires the policyholder to pay an initial premium for coverage. The deductible is a fixed amount the insured must pay out-of-pocket for repairs before the insurer covers the remaining costs. The deductible is usually chosen by the user at the time of purchasing the policy, with lower deductibles resulting in higher premiums and vice versa.

Initial Costs Involved:

  • Premium: Paid annually or semi-annually, depending on vehicle value, location, and coverage.
  • Deductible: Fixed at ₹5,000 to ₹15,000 based on user choice during policy purchase.

Detailed Case Study to Explain Deductible and Claim Settlement:

Scenario: You pay a ₹40,000 premium for comprehensive insurance with a ₹10,000 deductible.

Accident Repair Cost: ₹70,000.

Your Payment: ₹10,000 (deductible).

Insurer Payment: ₹60,000.

If the repair cost was ₹8,000, you would pay the entire amount, as it is below your deductible.

Insurance Policy Categories and Terms and Conditions

Comprehensive Insurance:

  • Coverage: Damages to own vehicle, third-party liabilities, theft, fire, natural disasters.
  • Premium: ₹20,000-₹50,000 annually.
  • Deductible: ₹5,000-₹10,000 depending on the chosen plan.
  • Benefits: Cashless claims, zero depreciation cover, roadside assistance, engine protection.

Third-Party Insurance:

  • Coverage: Third-party damage, injury, or death.
  • Premium: ₹7,000-₹15,000 annually.
  • Deductible: ₹5,000.

Own Damage Insurance:

  • Coverage: Own vehicle damage due to accidents, fire, theft.
  • Premium: ₹12,000-₹25,000 annually.
  • Deductible: ₹5,000-₹10,000.

Terms and Conditions:

  • Policy Period: One year, renewable.
  • No Claim Bonus: Discount of 20%-50% on premium renewal.
  • Cancellation Policy: Refund based on unused premium.

Claim Process:

  1. Notify Insurer: Within 24 hours.
  2. Submit Documents: Policy, photos, FIR (if applicable).
  3. Inspection: Vehicle inspection.
  4. Approval: Based on coverage.
  5. Settlement: Cashless repairs or reimbursement.
